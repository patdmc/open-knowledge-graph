### What Science Gets Wrong
- Trials are built on whats, not whys
- DFS methodology — each branch rigorous, no breadth pass
- The researcher learns the why, the community takes the what
- The what is portable (abstracts, citations, eligibility criteria), the why doesn't compound
- No one comes up for air to see that the branches are the same tree

### Structural Insights from Conversation
- **Discovery vs being told**: Discovery gives you the why. Being told gives you the what. On the field under pressure, the what isn't fast enough.
- **Every career step was the same problem**: QA (find failures by probing), functional composition (decompose), BA (translate between domains), MapReduce (partition and compress), coaching (encode axioms, let complexity emerge), GraphQL (composable graph traversal), knowledge graph (all of the above)
- **The coaching lesson transfers everywhere**: Can't make a team care more about winning than you. Can't script dynamic situations. Define axioms, maximize reps, let intelligence emerge. Same for orgs, same for cancer channels.
- **Incentive misalignment**: People promoted for shipping complexity, not removing it. Science funds new branches, not breadth passes. Stack ranking kills collaboration. The anti-graph.
- **"I'm kinda special too"**: Not just unique W'. He chose this path at every fork. The harder road. The one with more understanding. Agency, not accident.
- **TMB = "run this step 7 times"**: A process that works sometimes, nobody knows why, surrounded by contradictory backup studies. One missing variable clears it.
- **Checkout code as naked mole rat**: Simple, well-factored, unbeatable. 5 years unupdated, still winning on conversion. Simplicity IS the defense layer.
- **The graph is the breadth pass journalism should do**: Tricia's job was literally: source, confirm, publish free. The uncertainty merchants are dismantling those institutions.
- **Kripke / Hilo / naming**: "Channel count" didn't exist as a name. Without the name, nobody could reason about it as a variable. The name precipitates knowledge from fog into graph.
- **The two roads**: "Two roads diverged in a wood." Patrick always took the harder path — the one that led to more understanding. Now he's taking the other one — Tricia's road. The one where you make it public. Source it, confirm it, publish it free. The road less taken. And that has made all the difference.
