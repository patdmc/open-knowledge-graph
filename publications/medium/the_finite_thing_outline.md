# The Finite Thing — Revised Outline

First person. Short sentences. Patrick is McPhee — the storyteller riding through his own life, pointing at the geology his mentors/teachers/Tricia/doctors taught him. The reader learns it the way he learned it.

## I. Tricia
- How they met (2006, just after WhitePages). "I break big news stories." A1 of the Seattle Times.
- Who she was: Tech Tracks blog, PaidContent (2008, one of the first blogger journalists), AllThingsD, GeekWire, Bezos interview, UO Hall of Achievement
- "I can't believe I'm telling you this" — and then the scoop. She reveled in revealing secret truth.
- The complement: she was a researcher then interrogator, he was a predictor then validator
- In life it flipped — he guessed casually (the cardinal in Hawaii), she sourced everything
- She died August 30, 2024. Age 46. Three kids, four cities, seven drugs.
- The thesis: every piece of knowledge in this story arrived after the person who needed it was out of time.

## II. The Career as Car Ride
- Self-taught. Manual QA at RealNetworks — a 90s dinosaur profitable only from a $500M Microsoft antitrust settlement
- QA: decompose by probing blind. But could see in 2006 it was dying. Evolve.
- Business analyst at WhitePages ($13/hr, 29hrs/week, no insurance from first startup collapse, manager's wife hired him)
- The Oracle report: automated it, boss didn't believe the 5-minute version, made him do it manually. He had proof. Boss didn't.
- Billions of records, DVDs, "run this step 7 times, I don't know why but the data looks right"
- Stopped asking boss, started asking the software engineers
- Functional composition: decomposition from first principles. The root skill.
- Nathan Kriege: git, version control. The first graph — a DAG of commits.
- MapReduce: partition, compress, reduce. The math lesson — group by equivalence class first.

## XIV. Building the Graph
- RAG first. OK but unshareable.
- Walking career backwards: MapReduce → GraphQL → functional composition → pointers → file handles

- Space-covering problem = graph problem. m×n → (m+n).
- Equivalence-class-first ordering converges fastest. Proven.

## XV. Cancer as Engineering
- "I didn't believe the body was an unacting victim."
- Cancer is a runaway exception. Root cause analysis.
- Doing a post-mortem. Literally.
- The eight channels — textbook biology, engineering frame
- Assigned 92 genes. Counted channels per patient.
- The paradox: mutation count protective, conditional on channels. Didn't understand at first. Weeks to shake it out.
- Two classes: escalation vs passengers. 12-year debate resolved. One variable.
- The naming: "channel count" didn't exist. Kripke — the name fixes the reference. Without the name, no one could reason about it.
