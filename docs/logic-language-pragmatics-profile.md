## Overview

This cluster spans formal logic, semantic theory, pragmatist epistemology, and ecological psychology. What unites them: all five works address the relationship between a representation system and what it represents, and all five independently arrive at constraints that the UB framework derives from information theory.

Four emergent results trace to this cluster:
- **EM05**: Theorem 6 proves Peirce PC3 (only scientific inquiry survives)
- **EM11**: Peirce/Tarski → truth = action-relevance for any entity with A > 0 (fifth independent derivation of NV01)
- **EM08**: Gödel/Tarski → higher-order sentience is metalanguage possession
- **EC05** multiple derivations: Gödel G1, Tarski TA2, Chaitin CH2 all ground M < N independently

## Gödel (1931): Incompleteness as M < N

**G1 (First Incompleteness Theorem)**: any consistent formal system capable of expressing arithmetic contains statements that are neither provable nor refutable within it. G1 grounds EC05 (M < N is a logical consequence): any system capable of modeling W with sufficient expressiveness cannot fully represent W from within itself.

The UB framework's M < N bound (Theorem 3 Part II) is an instance of G1 applied to inference systems. The entity's K is a formal system; W is the domain. G1 says K cannot prove everything true of W — there are true statements about W that K cannot derive. M < N is not just practically difficult; it is logically necessary.

**G2 (Second Incompleteness Theorem)**: no consistent formal system can prove its own consistency. The entity cannot certify its own epistemic reliability from within its own K. This is the formal grounding for EM08: higher-order sentience (metalanguage possession) is required precisely because G2 prohibits self-grounding from within the object language. The entity that can evaluate its own K's reliability has achieved a metalanguage — a second formal system that evaluates the first.

**G3 (Gödel numbering)**: statements about a formal system can be encoded within it, enabling self-reference. This is the technical predecessor that Tarski extends to truth predicates, and that Hofstadter characterizes informally as strange loops.

## Tarski (1936): Truth, Metalanguage, and Provenance

**TA2 (Object/meta-language distinction)**: truth cannot be defined within the language it applies to — a metalanguage is required. This maps to the M < N structure: K cannot fully represent W from within K's own language. The Tarski undefinability theorem is a formal statement of why K always falls short of W.

**TA3 (Model-relative truth)**: truth is always relative to a model (interpretation). This grounds the provenance triple (D13): a proposition is true relative to the model established by (attribution, evidence, derivation). The provenance system is Tarski's model-relative truth operationalized: the provenance triple specifies the model.

**EM11 (Peirce + Tarski → truth = action-relevance)**: PC4 (pragmatic maxim: meaning = practical consequences) and TA3 (truth = model-relative) together establish that for an entity with A > 0, truth and action-relevance are the same criterion. The semantic model IS the action space. This is an independent derivation of NV01 from philosophy of language: the K/F graph (knowledge indexed by action space) is the unique truth-preserving structure.

## Peirce (1877, 1878): The Pragmatist Antecedent

**PC1 (doubt as driver)**: inquiry is driven by doubt — the unsettled state. U(w,K) > 0 is the formal version of doubt: the gradient signal for inquiry. Peirce (1877) is the philosophical antecedent of EC10 (uncertainty drives the entity).

**PC3 (only scientific inquiry survives)**: of the four methods for fixing belief (tenacity, authority, a priori reasoning, scientific inquiry), only scientific inquiry is stable against confrontation with W. This is EM05: Theorem 6 proves Peirce PC3 formally. Entities that do not genuinely engage W are removed by selection pressure. The formal theory replaces Peirce's qualitative argument with a derivation.

**PC4 (pragmatic maxim)**: a concept p is meaningful iff there exists at least one action f that yields different results depending on whether p is true. This is exactly the K/F grounding relation (Definition 3): p grounds f iff f's uncertainty depends on p. Propositions with no grounded actions have no practical meaning. PC4 is the philosophical grounding for Definition 3.

**PC5 (belief as habit of action)**: belief is a disposition to act. K retains propositions that are dispositions altering F. PC5 is the qualitative predecessor of K as propositions that ground F.

**PC6 (truth as long-run convergence)**: truth is the limit of inquiry — agreed upon by a community of inquirers given unlimited time. This maps to the M → N trajectory: truth is approached but only reached asymptotically. UB formalizes this as M < N: the approach never terminates for A > 0 entities.

**EM11 (Peirce PC4 + Tarski TA3)**: the pragmatic maxim and model-relative truth together yield a precise result — for any entity with A > 0, what is true and what is action-relevant are the same criterion. This is the fifth independent derivation of NV01: the K/F graph (K indexed by the action space F) is what truth requires when truth is action-relative and action is A > 0.

## Gibson (1979): Affordances and the Action-Relevance of Perception

**GI1 (affordances)**: perception is directly of action-relevant structure. The information perceived is already structured by what it enables. This maps to F: the informed action space encodes what is possible, not just what is observed. K/F grounding is Gibson's affordance relation formalized.

**GI2 (direct perception)**: Gibson claims no internal reconstruction of W is required — structure is in the optic array. This is in tension with the UB framework's necessity proof for K: Theorem 2b Part 3 proves that at bounded context, factored representation IS necessary. The tension resolves by scope: Gibson addresses perceptual coupling over short timescales; UB addresses learning and knowledge accumulation over extended timescales with bounded context.

**GI3 (ecological validity)**: W is an active, dynamic space, not a fixed dataset. This grounds the UB framework's treatment of W as a generative environment rather than a fixed signal.

**What Gibson contributes**: the most direct philosophical grounding for the K/F action-relevance relationship. Affordances ARE the F-grounding relation in ecological language. The K/F graph formalizes what Gibson leaves implicit: the propositions that give F its action-relevance structure.

## Cross-Cluster Convergence on EC05 and EC06

Three independent traditions independently establish that representation systems are necessarily incomplete relative to what they represent:
- **Gödel (G1)**: via formal arithmetic self-reference
- **Tarski (TA2)**: via truth predicates requiring metalanguage
- **Chaitin (CH2)**: via compression bounds on formal systems

And four independent traditions independently establish that the relevant representation is the action-relevant one:
- **Peirce PC4**: pragmatic maxim (meaning = practical consequences)
- **Tarski TA3**: model-relative truth (truth = relative to interpretation model)
- **Gibson GI1**: affordances (perception is of action-enabling structure)
- **UB EC06**: K grounds F (retained propositions are those that affect actions)

The convergence of three incompleteness derivations (EC05) and four action-relevance derivations (EC06) from different disciplines is the strongest structural evidence in the corpus for these two equivalency classes.
