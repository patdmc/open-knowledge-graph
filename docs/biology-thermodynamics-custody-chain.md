## REF-Schrodinger1944

**Citation status**: Verified (Cambridge University Press, 1944)

### SCH1 (negentropy) → D09 (survival threshold)

**Evidence grade**: B (physical grounding)

SCH1 grounds U_lethal as a thermodynamic threshold: below a certain negentropy extraction rate, the system cannot maintain structure. One step: "failure of negentropy extraction" must be identified with "U > U_lethal." Well-motivated — both describe the threshold below which the entity cannot maintain its organizational integrity — but SCH1 is a thermodynamic argument (entropy balance) while U_lethal is a formal epistemic parameter. The connection requires Jaynes' bridge (JA2: physical entropy = information entropy) to make the identification definitional rather than analogical.

**Relationship to EM13**: SCH1 is one of three physical groundings for EM13 alongside England (EN2) and Jaynes (JA2). JA2 is the formal bridge that makes all three definitional: once physical entropy = information entropy (Jaynes), SCH1's negentropy condition becomes the information-theoretic survival condition. The grade for SCH1 in isolation is B; the grade given EM13 is admitted is closer to A.

### SCH2 (aperiodic crystal) → EC08

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

The aperiodic crystal (non-redundant information storage) maps to the factored K requirement. One step: "aperiodic" (physical chemistry term for non-repeating structure) must be identified with "factored/non-redundant" (information theory term). Well-motivated — Schrödinger uses the information-theoretic argument explicitly — but the precise identity requires accepting that crystallographic aperiodicity and information-theoretic non-redundancy are the same property. Grade B.

**Independence**: Very strong. Schrödinger (1944) predates information theory and Shannon's formalism.

## REF-England2013

**Citation status**: Verified (DOI: 10.1063/1.4818538)

### EN2 (survival as energy dissipation) → D09

**Evidence grade**: B+ (physical derivation, stronger than Schrödinger)

England's result is stronger than Schrödinger's because it derives the survival condition from statistical mechanics rather than stating it as a biological observation. EN3 (Darwinian selection = thermodynamic favorability) establishes that the selection pressure in Theorem 6 has a physical derivation, not just a biological interpretation.

One step: "thermodynamic threshold below which self-replication cannot proceed" must be identified with "U_lethal." Well-motivated and England explicitly discusses the information-theoretic implications, but the formal identification still requires Jaynes' JA2 to be complete.

Grade B+ (stronger than SCH1's B): England derives the threshold from statistical mechanics; Schrödinger observes it. The derivation provides stronger formal grounding.

**Independence from UB**: Strong. England (2013) is statistical physics.

## REF-Prigogine1984

**Citation status**: Verified (Bantam Books, 1984; widely cited)

### PR2 (bifurcation at critical points) → Theorem 2b Part 3

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

The bifurcation structure of Theorem 2b Part 3 (two options at C_n: factoring preserves information, discard loses it) maps to PR2's critical point bifurcation (the system branches into qualitatively different states at the transition). One step: "critical point bifurcation in dissipative system" must be identified with "two-option boundary at C_n." Well-motivated — both describe a point at which the system must choose between qualitatively different trajectories, with no middle ground — but Prigogine's bifurcation is a physical phenomenon while the C_n boundary is an information-theoretic constraint.

**Unique contribution**: PR2 establishes that the bifurcation structure at C_n is not peculiar to information theory — it is a general physical phenomenon. Information-preserving and information-losing paths at C_n are instances of a broader class of thermodynamic bifurcations.

### PR3 (irreversibility) → encoding permanence

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

PR3 maps to the encoding permanence theorem (once a proposition is grounded, information gain is permanent). One step: "thermodynamic irreversibility" must be identified with "irreversibility of information gain in K." Well-motivated given Jaynes' JA2 bridge (physical entropy = information entropy), but requires the JA2 identification to be complete.

**Independence**: Strong. Prigogine and Stengers (1984) are thermodynamics and philosophy of science.

## REF-MaynardSmith1995

**Citation status**: Verified (Oxford University Press, 1995)

### MS2 (cooperation as transition) → Corollary 8a, D12

**Evidence grade**: B (structural, evolutionary grounding)

MS2 (the transition from selfish replicators to cooperating units is an information integration event) grounds Corollary 8a (K_collective intersection as information integration transition). One step: "major evolutionary transition through cooperation" must be identified with "K_collective formation as information integration event." Well-motivated — both describe the transition from independent entities to a coherent collective with emergent information properties — but the evolutionary transition involves physical/biological mechanisms while K_collective formation is a formal information-theoretic event.

**Unique contribution**: MS2 establishes that K_collective formation is not unique to human cognition — it is the general structure of every major evolutionary transition. The same pattern (independent replicators integrating into a coherent collective) has occurred multiple times in evolutionary history, which provides inductive evidence that EM10 (K_collective as unit of selection) represents a general attractor for complex systems.

### MS4 (unit of selection shifts) → EM10

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

MS4 grounds EM10 (K_collective as the relevant unit of selection) with evolutionary evidence. One step: "unit of selection shift at major transitions" must be identified with "K_collective as unit of selection replacing K_i." Well-motivated. The evolutionary pattern (unit of selection shifts upward with each major transition) is exactly what EM10 predicts for the transition from individual knowledge to collective knowledge.

**Independence**: Strong. Maynard Smith and Szathmáry (1995) do not discuss information-theoretic agent models.

## REF-Dunbar1992

**Citation status**: Verified (Journal of Human Evolution, 1992)

### DU1 (Dunbar's number ~150) → EM10 (empirical prediction)

**Evidence grade**: B+ (empirical, specific prediction)

DU1 provides an empirical measurement with a specific numeric prediction for K_collective coherence limit: approximately 150 for humans. One step: "maximum stable primate social group" must be identified with "maximum coherent K_collective at primate C_n." Well-motivated — both describe the limit at which the entity's tracking capacity for collective edges is exceeded — and the connection is explicit in the node's provenance.

Grade B+ (not A) because the identification of "social group" with "K_collective" requires one interpretive step. Social group size is a behavioral/ecological measure; K_collective size is a formal information-theoretic construct. The C_n for social relationship tracking is not the same formal object as C_n for proposition management.

**Unique contribution**: DU1 gives EM10's coherence limit a specific empirical value that the formal framework does not provide. The ~150 group size limit is a quantitative prediction for the scale at which K_collective coherence fails. No other node in the corpus provides this.

### DU2 (language as social grooming) → EM09

DU2 (language as low-cost K_collective maintenance) maps to EM09 (collective intelligence is thermodynamically favored). One step: "lower cost of language for relationship maintenance versus grooming" must be identified with "thermodynamic favorability of collective intelligence." ## REF-Axelrod1984

**Citation status**: Verified (Basic Books, 1984; widely cited)

### AX2 (cooperation conditions) → D13 + NV04 (potential emergent node)

**Evidence grade**: B+ (structural, near emergent)

Axelrod's three conditions for cooperation emergence (repeated interaction, recognition, reciprocity) are structurally parallel to the K_collective formation conditions:
- Repeated interaction = A > 0 over time
- Recognition = provenance tracking (D13)
- Reciprocity = K/F coupling

This is not just an analogy. Provenance (recognition in Axelrod's terms) is the formal requirement in both frameworks. K_collective forms when and only when the same three conditions hold as Axelrod requires for cooperation emergence. This convergence is from an entirely different theoretical direction: game theory, not information theory.

**Potential EM node**: AX2 + D13 + NV04 may warrant a named emergent node (EM-Axelrod or EM28): the game-theoretic conditions for cooperation emergence are the K_collective formation conditions. Provenance (D13) is the recognition condition without which neither cooperation nor K_collective can form. This would be the fourth independent derivation of the provenance requirement: (1) information-theoretic (DPI-based), (2) logical (Tarski TA3, model-relative truth), (3) thermodynamic (Jaynes JA1), (4) game-theoretic (Axelrod AX2).

Grade B+ for the current derivation (not A): the identification of "recognition" with "provenance" and "reciprocity" with "K/F coupling" are interpretive steps, though they are among the most strongly motivated in the cluster. If the potential EM is admitted, the grade for AX2's role in it becomes A.

### AX3 (defector invasion) → EM10 failure mode

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

AX3 (cooperating population invaded by defectors when provenance is lost) maps to EM10's failure mode (K_collective dissolves when provenance verification fails). One step: "defector invasion" must be identified with "K_collective dissolution." Well-motivated — both describe the mechanism by which a collective structure fails under individual defection — but AX3 is a game-theoretic result while EM10 is an information-theoretic claim.

**Independence**: Strong. Axelrod (1984) is game theory and political science.
