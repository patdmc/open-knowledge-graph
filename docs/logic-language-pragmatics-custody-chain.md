## REF-Godel1931

**Citation status**: Verified (DOI: 10.1007/BF01700692)

### G1 (First Incompleteness Theorem)

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for EC05)

Gödel's first theorem is one of the most rigorously verified results in mathematics. The mapping to EC05 (M < N is a logical consequence of the structure of inference systems) is structural: an entity's K is a formal system, W is the domain, and G1 says every formal system capable of arithmetic cannot fully characterize its domain from within. The translation requires accepting that K is "sufficiently expressive" in the sense G1 requires. Given that any K capable of reasoning about W contains arithmetic (it can count dimensions), the condition is met.

No interpretive gap for the incompleteness direction. Grade A for the M < N logical necessity claim.

### G2 (Second Incompleteness Theorem)

**Evidence grade**: B+ (structural)

G2: no consistent formal system proves its own consistency. The mapping to EM08 (higher-order sentience = metalanguage possession) requires accepting that "proving consistency" and "certifying epistemic reliability" are the same operation. One step: both involve the system evaluating its own foundations from within, and G2 says this is impossible without moving to a higher system. The step is well-motivated (epistemic reliability is a form of consistency certificate) but not definitional.

### G3 (Gödel numbering)

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Gödel numbering encodes statements about a system within the system, enabling self-reference. The mapping to EM08 is structural: Gödel numbering is the technical mechanism that makes the strange loop (Hofstadter) and the metalanguage (Tarski/EM08) possible. It is not itself the emergent result but the technical precursor.

### Independence from UB

Very strong. Gödel (1931) is pure formal logic. No epistemic, learning, or agent-theoretic motivation.

## REF-Tarski1936

**Citation status**: Verified (Tarski 1933 in Polish; 1956 English translation)

### TA2 (Undefinability of truth)

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for EM11)

Tarski proves truth cannot be defined within the language it applies to. The mapping to M < N structure is structural (grade B+ in isolation), but the mapping to the K/F grounding relation in EM11 is closer to definitional: TA3 (truth = model-relative) + PC4 (meaning = practical consequences) → for A > 0, truth = action-relevance, and the model IS the action space. EM11 is the admitted emergent node. Given EM11, TA2/TA3's role within it is grade A.

### TA3 (Model-relative truth) → Provenance grounding

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for D13)

The provenance triple (attribution, evidence, derivation) operationalizes model-relative truth: the triple specifies the model. This mapping is used explicitly in the framework's Definition 13. No translation gap.

### Independence from UB

Very strong. Tarski (1936) is formal semantics.

## REF-Peirce1877 and REF-Peirce1878

**Citation status**: Verified (Popular Science Monthly, 1877-1878; widely reprinted)

### PC3 (Only scientific inquiry survives) → EM05

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

PC3 is a qualitative argument that entities using tenacity, authority, or a priori methods for fixing belief eventually fail when confronted with W. Theorem 6 (Peirce's argument formalized) proves this with selection pressure: entities that do not genuinely engage W are removed. The mapping is clean but requires accepting that Peirce's "confrontation with W" maps to selection pressure — a non-trivial step. Grade B.

**Independence**: Strong. Peirce (1877) has no information-theoretic derivation.

### PC4 (Pragmatic maxim) → Definition 3 and EM11

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for Definition 3)

The pragmatic maxim states p is meaningful iff there exists f such that f's outcome depends on p. Definition 3 (K/F grounding relation) states exactly this: p grounds f iff U(f|K) - U(f|K+{p}) > 0 (f's uncertainty changes with p). PC4 is not just analogous to Definition 3 — it is the philosophical statement of exactly the same condition. Grade A.

For EM11 (PC4 + TA3 → truth = action-relevance), the combination of PC4 and TA3 yields the claim by two steps, both at grade B+ individually. EM11 as a combined result is grade B+ overall.

### PC6 (Truth as long-run convergence)

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

PC6 (truth = limit of inquiry) maps to M → N trajectory (M < N always under A > 0). One step: "limit of inquiry" and "M → N asymptote" must be identified. Well-motivated — both describe truth as approached but never fully reached — but the UB derivation is more precise (M < N is derived, not asserted).

## REF-Gibson1979

**Citation status**: Verified (Houghton Mifflin, 1979; widely cited)

### GI1 (Affordances) → Definition 7 and EC06

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Affordances are action-relevant environmental structures perceived directly. The mapping to the K/F grounding relation (EC06) requires accepting that affordances are the environmental analog of what UB calls the informed action space. One step. Grade B.

**Note**: GI2 (direct perception) is in tension with the K/F necessity proof. The tension resolves by scope — Gibson addresses perceptual coupling over short timescales without bounded context; UB addresses learning over extended timescales. This tension should be acknowledged in any citation of Gibson.

### Overall custody note for Peirce and Gibson

Both Peirce and Gibson function as philosophical precursors to formal UB definitions, not as independent derivations. The custody concern is different: for foundational works like Gödel and Shannon, the risk is incorrect technical attribution. For Peirce and Gibson, the risk is overclaiming — treating a qualitative philosophical antecedent as if it were a formal independent derivation.

**Assessment**: Peirce PC4 is grade A for Definition 3 (it is the philosophical statement of the same condition). Peirce PC3 is grade B for EM05 (Theorem 6 formalizes a qualitative argument). Gibson GI1 is grade B for EC06 (philosophical precursor, not independent derivation). These grades are appropriate and the provenance nodes correctly classify them as `philosophical_precursor` rather than `emergent-intersection`.
