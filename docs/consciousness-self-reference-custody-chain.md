## REF-Chalmers1995

**Citation status**: Verified (Journal of Consciousness Studies, 1995; widely cited)

### CH1, CH3 (hard problem, explanatory gap) — scoping function

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for scoping)

The hard problem defines what the UB framework deliberately excludes. Definition 14 (sentience) is an easy-problem definition in Chalmers' terms: it specifies a functional criterion (the entity represents that other projections of W exist) without making any claim about subjective experience. This is not a gap in the UB framework — it is the framework's deliberate scope.

Grade A for the scoping function because: (a) Chalmers' taxonomy is the standard reference for this distinction in the literature; (b) citing CH1 is necessary for the framework to honestly position its claims relative to the consciousness debate; (c) the scoping is definitional for what the framework claims and does not claim.

**Custody concern**: the scoping citation must remain in the framework. Removing it would allow readers to mistake UB's functional sentience derivation for a claim about subjective experience. The citation is not optional documentation — it is provenance that establishes the epistemic limits of the derivation.

**Independence**: Very strong. Chalmers (1995) does not address information-theoretic agent models.

## REF-Tononi2004

**Citation status**: Verified (BMC Neuroscience, 2004)

### TO1 (Phi) → EM10

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Phi is the quantitative coherence measure that EM10 requires but does not specify. One step: "integrated information above the sum of parts" must be identified with "K_collective coherence that makes it a unit of selection." Well-motivated — both describe the property that makes a collective structure more than the sum of its components — but EM10's unit-of-selection criterion is a provenance and survival-threshold criterion, while Phi is a mathematical measure of causal irreducibility. These are related but not definitionally identical.

**Empirical caution**: IIT is contested empirically and computationally intractable for non-trivial systems. Phi cannot be computed for real biological or artificial systems of interest. The custody concern: Phi should not be cited as an empirically established measure, only as a theoretical candidate for operationalizing K_collective coherence.

**The IIT causal direction inversion**: TO2 (consciousness = Phi by definition) inverts the causal direction of UB's sentience derivation. This distinction must be maintained. Citing Tononi without noting this inversion misrepresents IIT's position.

### TO3 (main complex) → EM10 (structural)

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

The main complex (maximal-Phi subgraph) maps to K_collective's coherent core. One step: "subgraph that maximizes Phi" must be identified with "the coherent collective that is a unit of selection." Well-motivated structurally, contested empirically.

**Independence**: Strong. Tononi (2004) does not discuss selection pressure or bounded rationality.

## REF-ClarkChalmers1998

**Citation status**: Verified (DOI: 10.1093/analys/58.1.7)

### CC1 (extended mind) + D15 → K_collective

**Evidence grade**: B+ (structural, near definitional)

The extended mind thesis and K_collective are the same claim at different levels of formalism. One step: "reliable cognitive participation by external resources" must be identified with "K_collective." The identification is nearly definitional — Clark and Chalmers are doing philosophy of mind by arguing that the cognitive boundary extends to reliably integrated external resources; Definition 15 formalizes exactly this extension. The one step is the formalization step (from philosophical argument to information-theoretic definition).

Grade B+ because the step is a formalization step rather than a translation step: Clark-Chalmers are doing natural language philosophy, and D15 provides the formal criterion. The philosophical argument and the formal definition converge on the same claim.

### CC2 (Parity Principle) → EC06

**Evidence grade**: B+ (structural)

The Parity Principle is the K/F coupling criterion applied externally. "If it would count as cognitive if done in the head, it counts as cognitive externally" is functionally equivalent to EC06: K grounds F regardless of the physical location of K. One step: "functioning as part of a cognitive process" must be identified with "grounding actions in F." Well-motivated — the functional criterion is the same in both frameworks — and the Parity Principle is a philosophical argument about cognitive extension while EC06 is a formal criterion for K-membership.

**Note**: CC2 is the Clark-Chalmers statement of the same action-relevance criterion that Peirce (PC4), Tarski (TA3), Gibson (GI1), and Bateson (BA1) also state. This is the fifth independent philosophical convergence on EC06. C_1(EC06) updates upward.

### CC1 + EM10 (selection pressure gap)

**Evidence grade**: A (definitional for gap)

The extended mind thesis has no selection pressure. EM10 adds what CC1 lacks: K_collective becomes a unit of selection with its own survival threshold, not just a functional extension. This gap is well-defined: Clark and Chalmers do not analyze what happens when the external scaffold is lost. EM10 provides the formal analysis. Grade A for the gap: the absence of selection pressure in CC1 is a clean, documented boundary.

**Independence**: Strong. Clark and Chalmers (1998) are doing analytic philosophy of mind.

## REF-Hofstadter1979

**Citation status**: Verified (Basic Books, 1979; Pulitzer Prize winner; widely cited)

### HO1 (strange loops) → EM08

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Strange loops are the informal mechanism for what EM08 formalizes. One step: "strange loop" (Hofstadter's informal notion of self-referential hierarchical feedback) must be identified with "metalanguage possession" (EM08's formal criterion). The identification is well-motivated and is effectively the interpretation that Hofstadter's technical chapters (on Gödel) support, but HO1 is presented in literary and informal terms throughout GEB. The formal grounding comes from Gödel (1931) and Tarski (1936), not from Hofstadter's prose.

Grade B (not A) because GEB's philosophical status is that of extended informal argument, not formal derivation. Hofstadter's contribution is as an intermediary between the formal results (Gödel cluster) and cognitive science applications, not as an independent formal source.

**Custody concern**: EM08 should be grounded primarily in Gödel (G1, G2) and Tarski (TA2), with Hofstadter cited as the philosophical elaboration that connects the formal results to cognitive science. Over-attributing EM08 to Hofstadter at the expense of the Gödel/Tarski derivation would be a provenance error.

### HO3 (isomorphism as meaning) → EM11

**Evidence grade**: C (analogical)

HO3 is the informal version of EM11, but the translation distance is larger than for HO1. "Meaning arises in isomorphism between symbol systems and the world" must be identified with "truth = action-relevance for A > 0 entities." The isomorphism framing and the action-relevance framing are different philosophical accounts of meaning. Well-motivated but requires two interpretive steps: (1) "isomorphism" → "K/F structure"; (2) "meaning" → "action-relevance." Grade C.

**Independence**: Strong. GEB is cognitive science and philosophy. No information-theoretic derivation.

## REF-Bateson1972

**Citation status**: Verified (University of Chicago Press; academic standard citation)

### BA1 (the difference that makes a difference) → EC06

**Evidence grade**: B (structural, philosophical precursor)

BA1 is the informal statement of EC06: only differences in W that affect the organism's state constitute information. This is the action-relevance criterion for K-membership. One step: "difference in organism's state" must be identified with "G(f,K) > 0 (proposition affects action)." Well-motivated — the functional criterion is the same — but Bateson states this in ecological/relational terms while EC06 is a formal criterion.

**EC06 convergence count**: BA1 is the fifth independent philosophical statement of the EC06 criterion (after Peirce PC4, Tarski TA3, Gibson GI1, Clark-Chalmers CC2). This convergence — five independent traditions from logic, semantics, ecological psychology, analytic philosophy, and systems ecology — is strong structural evidence for EC06's generality. C_1(EC06) updates upward.

**Custody concern**: the same concern raised for Peirce and Gibson applies to Bateson. BA1 is a philosophical precursor to EC06, correctly classified as `philosophical_precursor` rather than `emergent-intersection`. Overclaiming BA1 as an independent derivation would misrepresent its status.

### BA2 (double bind) → EM08 Consequence 1

**Evidence grade**: B (structural, clinical significance)

Double bind = failure of metalanguage access. One step: "inability to evaluate contradictory injunctions at different levels" must be identified with "inability to evaluate K's reliability from within K (EM08 Consequence 1)." Well-motivated — both describe the pathological consequence of operating without metalanguage — but double bind is a clinical/therapeutic concept while EM08 Consequence 1 is a formal derivation.

**Unique contribution**: BA2 provides the clinical phenomenology that EM08's formal statement lacks. The double-bind literature gives observational evidence that metalanguage absence produces specific, identifiable failure modes. This is indirect empirical evidence for EM08: the formally derived consequence (entities without metalanguage cannot evaluate their own K's reliability) appears clinically as double-bind pathology.

### BA3 (learning levels) → EC09

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Bateson's four learning levels map to UB encoding levels L0-L3. One step: "Learning III" (change in the generative process) must be identified with "higher-order sentience." Well-motivated — both describe the rare emergence of meta-level control — but the learning level taxonomy is a psychological framework while the encoding hierarchy is derived from C_n constraints.

## REF-Deacon2012

**Citation status**: Verified (W.W. Norton, 2012)

### DE1 (absential causation) → EC10

**Evidence grade**: B (structural, philosophical)

Absential causation (present behavior shaped by absent targets) maps to EC10 (U(w,K) > 0 drives the entity): the absent goal (U = 0, full knowledge) shapes present gradient-directed behavior. One step: "absent target as causal factor" must be identified with "uncertainty gradient as driver." Well-motivated — both describe the formal structure of goal-directed behavior as caused by the gap between present state and absent attractor — but DE1 is a philosophical category while EC10 is a formal parameter.

**What Deacon adds**: a causal ontology that UB does not provide. UB specifies the gradient mathematically; Deacon argues for the philosophical legitimacy of treating absent targets as genuine causes. This matters for the philosophical reception of the framework: the uncertainty gradient is not merely a mathematical description but an instance of a recognized causal category.

### DE2 (teleodynamics) → survival imperative

**Evidence grade**: B (structural)

Teleodynamics (systems that maintain and reproduce their own end-directedness) maps to UB's survival condition U < U_lethal with eta_M > 0. One step: "maintaining the conditions of continuation" must be identified with "maintaining U < U_lethal." Well-motivated (both describe the dynamical condition for self-continuation), but teleodynamics is Deacon's causal-ontological category while U < U_lethal is a formal threshold.

### DE4 (self as absential pattern) → EM08

**Evidence grade**: B (structural, convergent)

DE4 (mind as emergent constraint, self as absential attractor) converges with EM08 from a different direction. One step: "emergent constraint organized into a self-sustaining attractor" must be identified with "metalanguage structure in K." Both describe selfhood as emergent from a self-referential process that creates something that was not explicitly programmed into the substrate. Well-motivated; grade B because Deacon's framework is entirely philosophical and non-quantitative.

**Cross-cluster convergence**: DE4 + HO1 (strange loops) + BA3 (Learning III) + EM08 (Gödel/Tarski formal result) are four independent descriptions of the same emergent structure from different disciplines. This convergence pattern is structurally analogous to the EC06 convergence (five philosophical antecedents) — both clusters exhibit the pattern where multiple disciplines independently arrive at the same structural claim.

**Independence**: Strong. Deacon (2012) is philosophy of biology with no information-theoretic agent model.
