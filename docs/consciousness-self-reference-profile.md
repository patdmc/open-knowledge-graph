## Overview

This cluster spans consciousness philosophy, integrated information theory, extended mind theory, cognitive science, systems ecology, and philosophy of mind. What unites them: all six address the relationship between self-referential structure and awareness — how a physical system comes to model itself, its limits, and the world beyond those limits.

The UB framework makes a specific, scoped claim in this territory: functional sentience (Definition 14) is derivable from the information-theoretic structure, not axiomatic. The cluster's primary function is to establish what UB is and is not claiming relative to the consciousness literature, and to provide independent philosophical grounding for the framework's derived notions of metalanguage, collective knowledge, and action-relevant meaning.

## Chalmers (1995): Scoping the Framework's Claims

Chalmers distinguishes the easy problems of consciousness (explaining cognitive functions: attention, memory, learning, self-report) from the hard problem (explaining why physical processes give rise to subjective experience, qualia).

**CH1 (hard problem)**: there is an explanatory gap between third-person descriptions of cognitive processes and the first-person character of experience. Functional explanations leave the subjective character unexplained.

**CH2 (easy problems)**: explaining attention, memory, learning, and self-report are scientifically hard but conceptually tractable — they reduce to functional organization.

The UB framework's sentience definition (Definition 14) is an easy-problem definition in Chalmers' sense: an entity is sentient iff it represents that other projections of W exist. This is a functional criterion: it specifies what the entity's K must contain, not what it feels like to be the entity. The framework derives that functional sentience of this form follows from the information-theoretic structure under selection pressure. It makes no claim about CH1: why this functional organization gives rise to subjective experience remains an open question. Chalmers (1995) is cited to make this scope explicit.

The significance: UB's derivation of sentience removes functional sentience from the list of empirical assumptions. Chalmers' taxonomy establishes that this is the tractable half of the problem — and therefore that UB's derivation is philosophically significant even if CH1 remains open.

## Tononi (2004): Phi as K_collective Coherence

**TO1 (Phi)**: integrated information — the information generated by a system as a whole above and beyond the sum of its parts. Phi measures causal irreducibility: a system with high Phi cannot be decomposed without information loss.

Phi measures what UB calls K_collective coherence: the degree to which the collective's knowledge structure cannot be reduced to the sum of individual K_i. High Phi means K_collective is a genuine unit with its own information content; low Phi means K_collective is merely the concatenation of K_i without collective structure.

This gives EM10 (K_collective as unit of selection) a quantitative candidate. EM10 says K_collective becomes a unit of selection when it has its own coherence requirements (provenance verification across the collective). Phi operationalizes coherence: K_collective is a unit of selection when its Phi exceeds the threshold at which decomposition loses information.

**TO2 (consciousness = integrated information)**: the level of consciousness is its Phi. IIT defines rather than derives this. This inverts the causal direction of UB's Definition 14: IIT defines consciousness as Phi (no derivation); UB derives functional sentience from the information-theoretic structure. These are structurally distinct positions. IIT is contested empirically and computationally intractable for large systems.

**What Tononi contributes**: a quantitative candidate for the scalar that EM10 requires but does not specify. The main complex (TO3) — the subgraph that maximizes Phi — is K_collective's coherent core, and its identification as the unit of selection is structurally consistent with EM10's claim.

## Clark-Chalmers (1998): The Extended Mind as K_collective

**CC1 (extended mind thesis)**: mental states and cognitive processes can extend beyond the brain and body into the environment. When an external resource reliably functions as part of the cognitive process, it is part of the mind.

The extended mind is K_collective. An external resource (notebook, database, other person) that reliably participates in an entity's cognitive processing is part of the entity's effective K. Definition 15 (K_collective) is the formalization of the extended mind: K_collective is what the entity can reliably access, not just what is internal to it.

**CC2 (Parity Principle)**: if a part of the world functions in a process in a way that, if it were done in the head, we would count it as part of the cognitive process, then that part of the world is part of the cognitive process.

The Parity Principle is the K/F coupling criterion applied externally. A resource is part of K if it functions as part of F-grounding — if it is reliably used to make actions better. Whether the resource is internal or external is irrelevant to its epistemic role; what matters is EC06: K grounds F. This is the philosophical statement of exactly the K/F criterion.

**CC1 + D15 + EM10**: the extended mind and K_collective are the same claim at different levels of formalism. But EM10 adds what CC1 lacks: K_collective becomes a unit of selection with its own survival threshold. Organizations that lose their tools, records, and shared knowledge fail as collective units even if individual members persist. The extended mind thesis has no selection pressure; EM10 provides it.

## Hofstadter (1979): Strange Loops and the Informal EM08

**HO1 (strange loops)**: a strange loop occurs when, moving through a hierarchy of levels, you unexpectedly find yourself back where you started. Self-reference at sufficient complexity generates meaning from meaninglessness — the I emerges from strange loops.

Strange loops are the informal mechanism for what EM08 formalizes: the metalanguage that evaluates the object language is itself produced by the object language (through Gödel numbering). The I that observes K is produced by K. Higher-order sentience is the strange loop. GEB is the cultural/literary intermediary between Gödel's formal result and the cognitive science application of it.

HO1 + EM08 + G3 (Gödel numbering): the strange loop structure is the informal description of Gödel numbering applied at the cognitive level. EM08 says higher-order sentience is the metalanguage. Hofstadter says the I is the strange loop. These are the same claim: the entity that can refer to itself is one whose object language can model its own metalanguage.

**HO2 (tangled hierarchies)**: systems where levels are supposed to be cleanly separated instead loop back on themselves. Tangled hierarchies are the source of paradox, creativity, and consciousness.

Tangled hierarchy = the encoding level structure of UB under self-reference. L0-L3 are cleanly separated by assumption; when the entity models its own encoding levels (higher-order sentience), the hierarchy tangles. EC05 is the formal consequence: the tangled hierarchy produces incompleteness — the entity cannot fully model its own K from within K.

**HO3 (isomorphism as meaning)**: meaning arises in the isomorphism between symbol systems and the world, not in the symbols themselves. HO3 is the informal version of EM11: meaning is the isomorphism between K (symbol system) and W (world) mediated by F (actions). The structure-preserving map IS the K/F graph.

## Bateson (1972): Ecological Action-Relevance and Double-Bind Pathology

**BA1 (the difference that makes a difference)**: only differences in the environment that cause differences in the organism's state constitute information. Irrelevant differences are not information.

BA1 is the informal statement of EC06 (K grounds F): only dimensions of W that affect F are retained in K. The difference that makes a difference is G(f,K) > 0, the condition for retaining a proposition. Bateson's definition of information converges with Peirce's pragmatic maxim (PC4), Tarski's model-relative truth (TA3), Gibson's affordances (GI1), and Clark-Chalmers' Parity Principle (CC2). These are now five independent frameworks that arrive at the same action-relevance criterion for EC06.

**BA2 (double bind)**: a communication situation where the recipient receives contradictory injunctions at different logical levels with no ability to exit. Double binds produce pathological behavior — Bateson's analysis of schizophrenia as a failure of meta-communication.

Double bind = failure of metalanguage access. The entity cannot step outside the object language to evaluate the contradiction between levels. This is EM08's Consequence 1 (entities with only lower-order sentience cannot evaluate their own K's reliability) given a clinical face: double-bind pathology is the observable presentation of metalanguage absence. BA2 provides the clinical grounding that EM08's formal statement lacks.

**BA3 (learning levels)**: Learning 0 (no change), Learning I (change within fixed alternatives), Learning II (deutero-learning), Learning III (change in the learning process itself, rare and transformative). This maps directly to UB encoding levels L0-L3: Learning III corresponds to higher-order sentience — the entity changes the process that generates its alternatives, not just selects among them.

## Deacon (2012): Absential Causation and the Causal Ontology of U(w,K)

**DE1 (absential causation)**: causal processes driven by what is absent — by constraints, ends, purposes, values. Not all causation is present-to-present; the absent target shapes present dynamics.

DE1 gives EC10 (U(w,K) > 0 drives the entity) a causal ontology it does not specify. The entity's present behavior is shaped by an absent attractor (U = 0, full knowledge). The uncertainty gradient is absential causation: the entity is driven by the absent goal. Deacon provides the philosophical category for what UB formalizes as a gradient.

**DE2 (teleodynamics)**: systems that maintain and reproduce their own end-directedness. Life is teleodynamic: the organism works to preserve the conditions of its own continuation. Teleodynamics maps to UB's survival imperative: U < U_lethal is the condition for teleodynamic continuation, eta_M > 0 is the mechanism.

**DE4 (the self as absential pattern)**: mind is not a thing inside the brain but an emergent constraint organized into a self-sustaining attractor. The self is an absence made causally effective.

DE4 + EM08 + HO1 (strange loops) + Tarski TA2 (metalanguage): four frameworks converge on the same claim about higher-order selfhood. Hofstadter: the I is the strange loop. Tarski/Gödel: the metalanguage is a second formal system that evaluates the first. Bateson: Learning III is the change in the generative process. Deacon: the self is an absential pattern — a constraint shaped by absent ends. All four are describing the same emergent structure from different disciplinary angles. EM08 is where these converge formally.

Four independent philosophical and scientific traditions describe higher-order selfhood/metalanguage:

Peirce PC4, Tarski TA3, Gibson GI1, Clark-Chalmers CC2, Bateson BA1 — all from different disciplines, all arriving at: information is what affects action.

## What This Cluster Does Not Provide

The cluster does not independently derive any UB formal result. No proposition here carries a grade-A assignment for a UB derivation step. The cluster functions as:

1. **Philosophical scoping** (Chalmers): establishes what UB claims and does not claim relative to the consciousness debate
2. **Convergent philosophical grounding** (all five): independently-motivated descriptions of the same structures the UB framework derives
3. **Failure mode documentation** (Bateson BA2, Tononi TO3): what the absence of these structures looks like in practice
4. **Causal ontology** (Deacon): provides the causal-philosophical language for UB's gradient mechanics
